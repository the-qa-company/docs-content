You might have experienced searching for a particular question on a website and then going through the entire page to find the answer. This is a very tedious task and can be easily automated using a AI Assistant. 

:::tip
Sometimes searching information through a website can be very tedious. So why don't you try our AI Assistants over websites?
Let's see how do to it!
:::

In this section we will learn how to create a website based AI Assistant.

<Image src='/img/screenshots/tutorial-ui/data-source/website/website-empty.png' alt='Empty wizard'/>

## Handle sub-pages
### Delete sub-pages

Some sub-pages might not interest you, you can remove them by clicking on the delete button (in the *Action* column).

<Image src='/img/screenshots/tutorial-ui/data-source/website/website-remove.png' alt='Remove'/>

### Move sub-pages

Your sub-pages are originally grouped but you can move them **out of their group** by clicking on the *Move* button (in the *Action* column).

## Creation
Once you picked the name, language of your AI Assistant and filtered your links, you can click on:
* <kbd>Settings</kbd> to personnalize them (default parameters are good for general use)
* <kbd>Finish</kbd> to create your AI Assistant (and use default parameters)

## Settings (optional)

Then you can customize your QA System parameters if needed (default parameters are good for general use).

<Image src='/img/screenshots/tutorial-ui/data-source/website/creation-settings.png' alt='Settings'/>

After clicking on the <kbd>Finish</kbd> button, you will be redirected to the home page and you
will have your newly built QA system appearing in the list.
